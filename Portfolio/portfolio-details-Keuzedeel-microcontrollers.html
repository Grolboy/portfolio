Keuzedeel Micro controllers

Working with selectable components of microcontrollers proved to be an enjoyable and surprisingly straightforward undertaking. During this period, I learned how powerful and versatile microcontrollers can be, and how you can realize impressive projects with relatively simple means. Each lesson was different, from understanding the basics to actually building working prototypes. It was delightful to observe the swiftness with which one can achieve progress and how small steps ultimately lead to a comprehensive and functional system.

The highlight of the course was, without a doubt, the final project. This project was designed specifically to help people choose colors for websites based on RGB values. With three potentiometers, you could adjust the red, green, and blue values, which were then displayed on an RGB LED. This LED immediately displayed a mixed color, which allowed you to see the effects of your adjustments in real-time. This project was not only useful, but also enjoyable. The challenge was to fine tune the potentiometers and program the microcontroller to display the correct colors.

Despite the apparent complexity of the project, it was completed within a span of 3 to 4 hours. This was partly due to the step-by-step guidance and clear instructions we received during the course, which we appreciated. Every step felt manageable, and the end result was visually appealing and functionally useful.

For my entire portfolio, I spent 30 hours, during which I included several smaller projects and exercises. These projects have provided me with a comprehensive comprehension of the operation and capabilities of microcontrollers. I also devoted an additional 20 hours to a specialized car project, which provided additional depth and further refined my abilities.

In conclusion, collaborating with microcontrollers proved to be a highly satisfying experience. It was not only educational, but also enjoyable. It is a great activity for anyone interested in technology and programming because of the simplicity with which you can turn ideas into working prototypes.
