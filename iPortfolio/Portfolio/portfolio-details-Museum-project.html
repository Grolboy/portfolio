Museum Hindeloopen Educational game project

During a two-school-period-long project, I collaborated with a team of five individuals on the development of an educational game for the Museum of Hindeloopen. Within the team, I served as one of three developers. Throughout the project's duration, we diligently worked to refine our product, resulting in a robust offering by the time of the demonstration day.

Receiving an abundance of positive feedback during the demonstration, along with several requests for additional features, our team seized the opportunity to enhance the game further. One notable request was to make the game semi-portable, facilitating its use in educational settings such as schools, thereby enabling the dissemination of knowledge about Hindeloopen's history.

Upon receiving this directive from our scrum leader, a colleague and I undertook the task of constructing the portable unit. Armed with a blueprint at the outset of our endeavor, we swiftly progressed from prototyping to refining the design. Each successive day brought tangible improvements, culminating in the completion of the box by the third day, with only the final touches of paint remaining on the fourth day.

In the eleventh hour before the presentation to the museum, we encountered a technical issue with a component of the box. Despite the time constraints, we managed to address and rectify the issue just moments before the presentation, ensuring a flawless end product. The museum's representatives were thoroughly impressed, awarding our team a commendable rating of 9 out of 10 for our efforts.
